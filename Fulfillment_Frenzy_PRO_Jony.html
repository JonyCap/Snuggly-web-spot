Fulfillment Frenzy

Understanding Real-World Challenges in Order Fulfillment & Distribution

Stage Chosen:

Order Fulfillment & Distribution (Outbound)

Problem Research

What’s the Issue?
Order fulfillment is one of the most time-sensitive and customer-facing stages in global logistics—especially in e-commerce. With rising demand for rapid delivery, companies struggle to deliver quickly and accurately, particularly during flash sales or peak seasons.

Why Is It Happening?

  • Poor coordination between warehouses and couriers
  • Outdated systems and tech infrastructure
  • Unpredictable and expensive last-mile delivery
  • Understaffing or limited automation during spikes
  • Errors in picking, packing, or inventory communication

Real Examples:

  • Amazon adds temp staff and centers during Prime Day
  • Zara uses centralized hubs – delays in Spain affect global flow
  • Small sellers on Etsy struggle with DIY fulfillment

Consequences:

  • Late or incorrect deliveries
  • Low reviews and customer loss
  • High costs from returns/refunds
  • Burnout and higher error rates
  • Brand damage and lost trust

Learning Activity Proposal

Problem Statement:
How can logistics teams maintain speed, accuracy, and customer satisfaction in order fulfillment—even during extreme demand surges like flash sales or global campaigns?

Activity Title: Fulfillment Frenzy: Global Dispatch Crisis

Concept Overview:
This is a web-based interactive simulation game where students take the role of fulfillment managers during a global mega-sale. They’ll face real-world constraints and make split-second decisions to deliver success.

Student Tasks:

  • Design warehouse layout using drag-and-drop zones
  • Assign roles: pickers, packers, QA, dispatch
  • Set and track KPIs (speed, cost, satisfaction)
  • Respond to live disruptions: delays, breakdowns, weather
  • Balance in-house vs 3PL fulfillment options

Tools & Data Used:

  • Google Sheets for KPI dashboards
  • Web app sliders and layout builders
  • Scenario cards introducing unpredictable events

Why It’s Useful:
This immersive activity mimics real-world pressure, improves quick decision-making, and strengthens logistics thinking under demand chaos.

PRD (Product Requirements Document):

  • Platform: Browser-based (HTML/JS)
  • Players: 1–4 per team
  • Duration: 20–30 minutes
  • Scoring: Efficiency, delivery speed, customer happiness
  • Outcome: End-game analytics compared to industry benchmarks
